Penne Pasta Recipe

History

There are many theories to where pasta originally came from. Some say it was first made in Italy, but that's not true. Pasta was first made in China in 1700-1100 BC during the Shang Dynasty. This was confirmed in 2005 when archaeologists found a 4000 year old bowl of pasta in Lajia, China. Pasta in China back then was made from wheat or rice flour. Other places like Greece and Africa also have differnet variations of pasta. Pasta was first brought to Italy when Marco Polo came back from his trip to China on the Silk Road. He tried it in China and liked it so much that he decided to bring it back to Italy with him. Today pasta from Italy is made from durum wheat because it is easy to store and doesn't go bad.

Penne was first made in Liguria, Italy in the 19th century when a person named Giovanni Battista Capurro made a machine that could cut pasta without breaking it. This allowed people to make all kinds of shapes of pasta including penne. Penne is a hollow cylinder-shaped noodle with pointed ends. The sides of the pasta can either be smooth or grooved.

This recipe will tell you the best way to cook premade penne pasta. This recipe is easy, quick, and doesn't require tons of ingredients to make it.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 Quarts of Water
  • Salt
  • 1 Box of Barilla Penne Pasta

Directions

  1. Pour 4-6 quarts of water in a pan and bring to a boil. Add a pinch of salt.
  2. Add box of penne pasta and stir gently.
  3. Return to a boil and occasionally stir pasta for 11 minutes.
  4. Remove from stove and drain water.
  5. Serve penne pasta with any sauce you would like.
